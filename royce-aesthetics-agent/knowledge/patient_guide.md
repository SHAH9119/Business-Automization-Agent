## First Visit — What to Expect

1. **Reception check-in** — name, phone number, new/returning patient
2. **Brief history** — skin/hair concern, medications, allergies, pregnancy status if relevant
3. **Dermatologist consultation** — 15–30 minutes for assessment
4. **Treatment plan discussion** — options, sessions needed, estimated cost, downtime
5. **Treatment (if same day)** — some procedures (HydraFacial, laser, peels) can be done same day if time allows; transplants and injectables may need a separate slot

**Duration guide:**

| Visit Type | Typical Duration |
|---|---|
| Consultation only | 20–30 minutes |
| Consultation + HydraFacial | 60–75 minutes |
| Laser session (small area) | 15–30 minutes |
| Laser session (large area / full body) | 45–90 minutes |
| Chemical peel | 30–45 minutes |
| Botox / fillers | 30–45 minutes |
| Hair PRP | 45–60 minutes |
| Hair transplant (FUE/DHI) | 4–8 hours (full day) |

## Before Treatment — General Guidelines

*(Agent shares general info only — not personalized medical advice.)*

- Avoid sun exposure and tanning 1–2 weeks before laser or peel treatments
- Inform the clinic if pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning pregnancy
- Inform the clinic about diabetes, blood thinners, or known allergies
- For injectables: avoid blood-thinning supplements (fish oil, aspirin) unless doctor says otherwise — **patient must confirm with dermatologist**
- For laser hair removal: shave the area 24 hours before (do not wax or pluck for 4–6 weeks prior)

---

## After Treatment — General Aftercare

*(General only — specific aftercare is given by the clinic.)*

- Use sunscreen SPF 30+ daily after peels, laser, and most skin treatments
- Avoid direct sun for 48–72 hours after laser or peels
- Mild redness or swelling after injectables is normal for 24–48 hours
- Do not pick at peeling skin after chemical peels
- Follow-up appointments are important for PRP, laser, and peel courses
