# Services And Conditions

Royce Aesthetics publicly mentions treatment categories including skin treatment, acne treatment, hair loss treatment, and aesthetic beauty services.

- Acne
- Rosacea
- Pigmentation disorders
- Hair and nail disorders
- Melasma and freckles
- Xanthelasma
- Viral warts
- Eczema
- Seborrheic dermatitis
- Contact dermatitis
- Psoriasis
- Urticaria
- Mouth ulcers
- Blistering disorders
- SLE
- Systemic sclerosis
- Sarcoidosis
- Cutaneous vasculitis
- Skin ulcers
- Cutaneous drug reactions
- Cutaneous cysts
- Nevus
- Bacterial, viral, fungal, and parasitic skin infections

## Aesthetic And Hair Services Mentioned Publicly

- Hair transplant
- Non-transplant hair restoration
- Hair threads
- Hair fillers
- Micro-pigmentations
- Laser hair removal
- Smooth Face
- Beautiful Lips
- Chic Look
- Healthy Skin
- No Mole
- Soft Skin

These last six names are broad labels used on the public website. The website does not explain the exact procedure included under each label, so staff should confirm details.

## Demo-Only Offerings

The following are included only to test the prototype. They are not explicitly listed on the clinic's public website:

- Hydrafacial
- Hair PRP

The agent must call these demo offerings and must not claim that the clinic officially provides them.

## Not Publicly Listed

- Waxing
- Full-body waxing

If asked about an unlisted service, the agent should say it is not listed publicly and offer staff confirmation instead of guessing.

## How The Agent Should Talk About Services

The agent can say:

"Royce Aesthetics publicly mentions acne, pigmentation, hair loss, laser hair removal, hair restoration, and other skin or aesthetic services. I can collect your concern and preferred appointment time so staff can confirm the best option."

The agent should not say:

"You need this treatment."

"This will cure your condition."

"This treatment is guaranteed."

"This price is confirmed."
